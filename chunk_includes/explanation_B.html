Hello, and thank you for your participation!

Amy, Bob and Chris are playing a memory game with cards. Each round, they get to see a different card, but only for a few seconds. All cards consist of six cells, and each cell can contain one of various symbols

The aim is to remember where certain symbols appeared on the card. Which symbols need to be remembered varies from round to round and is indicated on the card. If a player manages to recall the position of the relevant symbols correctly, she gets 5 dollars. If she makes any mistakes, she loses 10 dollars.

Players MUST give an answer each round. Even when they aren't sure of the answer, they have to make a guess about each symbol.

What you will see are the actual cards, and how Amy, Bob and Chris remember them. Using this information, you will have to answer various questions about Amy, Bob and Chris.

Who will win 5 dollars in this round?

Here, you would select Chris and possibly Bob. We can't know for sure whether Bob will win 5 dollars: he is unsure about the two upper symbols and will therefore have to guess what they are. If he guesses that one or two of those symbols are circles, he will lose 10 dollars. But if he guesses that they are something else than circles, he will win 5 dollars. So, there is a real chance that Bob will make the right guess.

On the other hand, the two other players have no doubts about the card and we know for sure what they will answer. We can thus tell that Chris will win 5 dollars and that Amy will lose 10 dollars.

Here are some final recommendations before you start the experiment:

  • Always select all the correct answers before moving to the next item.
  • In some cases you might be unsure which answers to select. If this happens, follow your intuition!
  • The experiment will take about 15 minutes.
